The Process Philosophy

The Big Mistake

Jumping to solutions too early. This is the #1 killer of creative problem solving.

The Golden Rule

"A problem fully understood is half solved."

Creativity is not just chaos; it is a structured, disciplined process.

PHILOSOPHYSLIDE 02 / 11
STAGE 1

Problem Exploration

01
Objective: Understand the real problem (Root Cause), not just the symptom.
  • Clarify Constraints: What are the boundaries?
  • Root Causes: Ask "What exactly is wrong?"
  • Crucial Rule: Do NOT generate solutions here.
  • Time Allocation: Spend the MAXIMUM time here.
"If I had 6 hours to cut a tree, I'd spend 4 sharpening the axe."

EXPLORATIONSLIDE 03 / 11
STAGE 2

Solution Generation

02

"Solution Vegetation"

Objective: Generate as many ideas as possible. Quantity leads to Quality.

The 3 Rules:
1. No Judgment.
2. No Criticism.
3. No Filtering.

Example: 550 raw ideas → refined into 50 strong ideas.

The Lego Principle

More blocks (ideas) = More possible combinations.

"Like boiling milk: Messy but necessary."
GENERATIONSLIDE 04 / 11
STAGE 3

Solution Validation

03

"Skimming the Cream"

Objective: Select the BEST idea, not the most comfortable one.

This is where we shift from creativity to analysis.

  • Key Shift: Logic > Intuition.
  • Tools: Data, Reasoning, Feasibility checks.
  • Methods: Compare alternatives, Triangulate with others, Test assumptions.

COGNITION

Two Distinct Thinking Modes

1. Divergent Thinking

(Used in Stage 2: Generation)

  • Purpose: Create multiple possibilities.
  • Nature: Expands thinking, encourages originality.
  • Methods: Brainstorming, Wild ideas, Multiple viewpoints.
  • Motto: "Yes, and..."

2. Convergent Thinking

(Used in Stage 3: Validation)

  • Purpose: Narrow down to the best solution.
  • Nature: Logical, Analytical, Evaluative.
  • Methods: Selection, Constraints, Feasibility.
  • Motto: "Does this work?"
THINKING MODESSLIDE 06 / 11
CRITICAL EXAM POINT

The Correct Sequence

You cannot drive with the brakes on. You cannot expand and contract at the same time.

❌ WRONG:
Convergent before Divergent.
(Kills Creativity)
✅ RIGHT:
Divergent FIRST → Convergent LATER.
(Effective Creativity)
SEQUENCESLIDE 07 / 11
SKILL #1

Listen with Intent

Listening is a magnet skill for creativity. Use the RASA Framework.

R - Receive

Listen without judgment or filtering. Absorb raw data.

A - Appreciate

Acknowledge the speaker (Nods, "Hmm"). Does not mean agreement.

S - Summarize

Repeat key points to ensure clarity. "So what you're saying is..."

A - Ask

Ask empathetic questions.
Say: "What happened?"
Don't Say: "Why did you do this?"

SKILL #2

Observe with Purpose

The Attention Rule

"You observe only what your attention allows."

Creativity requires intentional observation, not just passive seeing.

  • The Enemy: Screens reduce peripheral awareness.
  • The Fix: Look up. Look around. Notice details.
  • The Benefit: Observation feeds the "Expertise" component of creativity.

DEFER
JUDGMENT

Judgment is the enemy of learning and creativity.

Oscar Wilde:

"Every saint has a past, and every sinner has a future."
SKILL #3

Why Defer?

1. It stops situations from unfolding.
You force reality into your existing mental templates.
2. Regret.
People regret judging too early more than judging too late.

Example: Stephen Covey

A father on a train let his noisy kids disturb everyone. People judged him as careless.
The Reality: His wife had just died an hour ago, and they didn't know how to handle it.
Context changed the entire interpretation.
